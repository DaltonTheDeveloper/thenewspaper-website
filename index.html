The goal isn’t to chase every outrage cycle. It’s to give you a grounded, reality-based sense of what changed today — in Congress, the courts, the White House, and the broader system.

Under the hood, we use an AI research assistant to sift through real reporting across outlets and wire services. Then we apply a fixed set of rules so the daily briefing is consistent, transparent, and bias-aware.

You’ll always know what’s signal vs. what’s noise, and you’ll never have to doomscroll Twitter to get there.

How the briefing works

Rules the algorithm has to follow

  • Source-first: Summaries are always tied to specific pieces of reporting (with links).
  • Cross-spectrum: We sample outlets across the political spectrum and from straight-news desks.
  • No vibes-only narratives: If it’s just a take, it doesn’t lead the briefing.
  • Bias-aware: We track which outlets we’re leaning on and balance accordingly.

Built for people who follow politics casually but want to stay grounded in real reporting — not just the loudest timelines.
